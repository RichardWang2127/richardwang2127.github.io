Conflict

The central conflict of the story is Richard versus Society (and his own self-worth) . He is a bright student with top grades in math and science and a high contribution to the community, yet he is rejected for a scholarship based on his "comprehensive scores" (GPA). This external conflict creates an internal one, where Richard begins to internalize this failure, believing his value is diminished because a committee and a number said so.
Plot Diagram

Exposition
We are introduced to Richard, a 14-year-old boy with strong skills in mathematics, science, and coding. He has developed a club attendance system for his school and is awaiting the results of his scholarship application, which he is confident about.

Rising Action

    Richard's excitement turns to devastation when he reads the rejection email, which cites insufficient "comprehensive scores" (GPA).

    His friend Mango finds him distraught. Wanting to protect Richard from a harsh truth, Mango lies, blaming the rejection on fierce competition and his grades in other subjects.

    In the following days, Richard's demeanor changes drastically. He becomes hollow and disengaged, quitting his extracurricular activities and taking his personal website offline. Mango realizes his well-intentioned lie has had the opposite effect, becoming a "cage" that has crushed Richard's spirit.

Climax
Mango confronts the depressed Richard. Overcome with guilt and emotion, Mango abandons the lie and tells the truth. He reveals that the competition wasn't the issue and that Richard is successful. He explicitly names the true antagonist: the scholarship's "monstrous rubric" and GPA—a "number" that fails to measure Richard's actual value. Hearing the words "scholarship" and "GPA" acts as a trigger, causing Richard to break down in desperate sobs, releasing all his pent-up pressure.

Falling Action
Mango watches his friend's emotional breakdown, understanding the damage the obsession with the numbers has caused. He offers comfort with a warm hug. After the storm of emotion passes, Richard composes himself. His eyes are red, but they now hold a "new clarity."

Resolution
Richard has reframed his understanding of the situation. He now recognizes the "enemy" as the flawed system of measurement (GPA), not himself. With a sad but soft smile, he finds peace in the realization that his worth is no longer defined by a numerical table.
